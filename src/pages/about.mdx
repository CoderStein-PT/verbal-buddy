## Why?

There are many different reasons why you are struggling with communication.

One of them might be that you simply don't use your speaking language enough.

You sit all day long in front of your computer and don't talk to anyone.

You're not accessing the part of your brain responsible for human communication (if you're a hardcore computer programmer, for instance)
so it kinda atrophies as the time goes by and you lose the ability to communicate well.

Your main language becomes Python or JavaScript and you even become good at it.
But guess what are you becoming bad at?

Human communication.

### And that's not trivial!

It's a crucial skill as all success in our civilization is based on human communication - from making friends to getting a job.

It's no wonder that people who are not super-smart but good at communicating feel more fulfilled, confident and relaxed in life than those who are smart but lack communication skills (just look around!).

Your confidence gets low because: - You know that you'll likely to stutter and forget words in social situations. - And in situations where your life depends on what you will say (like a job interview, presentation, sales, relationships, etc.) and everything moves quickly, you'll just freeze or say something stupid. - In negotiations/conflicts instead of thinking about the context of it, you spend time forgetting/remembering **the words/phrases all the time** and the person who thinks more about the context, thinks and speaks faster **has much more leverage and advantage.** - So you avoid those situations and feel like you're missing out on life.

And because of that you might feel anxious, depressed, lonely, etc. So it's a vicious cycle...

That might be already enough for you to: - Have more control over your relationships and life - Keep up with the tempo of conversations and control them better - Be less anxious, more confident and relaxed - Make others hear your ideas and actually make a difference instead of being a bystander - Make friends and start relationships easier - Get a job/promotion/open a business/raise money/hire people etc. - Don't have awkward silences anymore. - Actually having fun.

But there is a deeper level: - Describe objects/ideas/feelings/life quickly and find relations between them. - Having quick brain access to your thoughts and ideas.

..will unlock another level which will allow you to: - Be funny when it's needed or wanted - Be creative and tell interesting and captivating stories - Be a good speaker, presenter, negotiator, leader, teacher etc. - Others will benefit too - not just you! ## How it works?

Reading books and watching movies is not enough. You need to create. You need to be the one who is expressing - not just consuming.

Just hanging out with your friends or partying also isn't enough - you spend a lot of time waiting and listening to others instead of actually improving.

You need to proactively practice accessing the words/phrases/sentences and make your brain strengthen the pathways leading to them so you always have them at your fingertips to use in any situation.

## How to use the app?

This app provides a few tools to help you practice - but doesn't do it for you (it's supposed to be like that since being proactive is the highest priority here).

So there are a few sections:

### Content

Here you can create a list of words/phrases you want to learn. You have a few presets in the settings to get you started. But it's recommended to create your own list.

To create your own list just think of a category of things in life (like food, travel, work, relationships, hobbies, feelings, body, animals, cities, etc.) and create it here.

Then think of words/phrases that belong to that category and add them to it.

Don't rush - you can always come back and add more words/categories later.

Now go back to categories and click on the "practice" button next to the category you want to practice. You'll have to fill in a certain amount of words you remember. When you finish the results will get saved to the statistics section. You'll have to improve the time you spend thinking about the words (Average Delay time) which is what usually matters the most and must be a good metric to measure your progress.

You can also add descriptions to words which is also a good practice by itself. It will help you think deeper about the words and their relations.

You can relate words to each other (for instance "dog" is a "pet").

You also can create the opposite descriptions of words (for instance "sun" -> "moon").

This will be useful in the "Jokes" section below.

### Guess

Here you can practice guessing words/phrases from their descriptions from the categories you created.

### Jokes

In this section you can practice jokes by using a method called "analogy" with the words you created in the "Content" section.
What makes people laugh is when you make a connection between two things that are not usually connected.

For instance:

> What do you call a cow with no legs?
>
> Ground beef.

To make those kinds of jokes more easily and quickly you just take two words and list their qualities. For instance:

## How much time will it take?

It's a tool to help you practice, not a magic pill that will make you a genius in a week.
Try to be consistent and practice frequently.

Don't get discouraged if you don't see results immediately - it's a skill that takes time to develop.
